live fire · the service keeps dying

You're paged with what production shows you and nothing more. Drive real tools; score on the evidence you point at, not on a guess.

📟 PAGE. A service, svc, keeps dying under load — no crash, no error in its own logs, it's just gone, and the supervisor restarts it in a loop.

It runs fine on a smaller workload, and fine on the dev laptop. Same binary, same inputs. Something outside the app is killing it. Find out who, and why.

Don't open the post-mortem until you've driven it.

[01]

$ RUN THE SCENARIO

make check LESSON=wheel/mystery-oom-kill

This boots the broken guest: the service runs inside a cgroup with a small memory limit, grows past it, and gets reaped. The app's source is fine — the bug is in the environment's memory budget.

[02]

./YOUR JOB

  1. Reproduce & read the symptom. The service vanishes mid-stride; its own output just stops. Nothing in the app explains it.
  2. Ask the kernel. When the kernel kills a process to reclaim memory, it leaves a detailed report in dmesg (dmesg | grep -i oom).
  3. Read the OOM report. Who got picked (process, pid), how much it was using (anon-rss), and — crucially — the constraint.
  4. Point at the evidence. "svc was OOM-killed by its cgroup limit (constraint=CONSTRAINT_MEMCG); memory.max=16M, its RSS hit the cap."
[03]

./READ THE OOM REPORT

hog invoked oom-killer: gfp_mask=0x..., order=0, oom_score_adj=0
memory: usage 16384kB, limit 16384kB, failcnt N
...
oom-kill:constraint=CONSTRAINT_MEMCG,...,task=hog,pid=N
Memory cgroup out of memory: Killed process N (hog) total-vm:..., anon-rss:..., oom_score_adj:0

constraint=CONSTRAINT_MEMCG is the tell: the whole machine didn't run out of memory — this process's cgroup hit its limit. That's the difference between "buy more RAM" and "raise the container's limit (or fix the workload)."

[04]

./GRADUATED HINTS

where's the evidence?

The app logged nothing because it didn't fail — it was killed. Ask the kernel: dmesg | grep -i oom (or look for Killed process).

global or cgroup?

Look at constraint=. CONSTRAINT_NONE = the whole box ran out; CONSTRAINT_MEMCG = a cgroup limit was hit — only this service's memory was capped.

the limit 
cat /cg/svc/memory.max      # the cap: 16M
cat /cg/svc/memory.events   # oom_kill count

The working set grew past 16M; the limit is too small for the workload.

[05]

./POST-MORTEM

open after you've driven it — ground truth + the lesson

Ground truth. The service runs in a cgroup with memory.max = 16M. Its working set grows past 16M; the cgroup OOM killer reaps the biggest task in the cgroup (the service). Nothing is wrong with the binary — the limit is too small for the workload.

Why it's the archetype. Containers ship with memory limits; the same image that's fine on a laptop (no limit) gets OOM-killed in prod (a tight memory.max). It only makes sense once you read the OOM report and notice the cgroup constraint — not "the box is out of RAM," but "this service's cap was hit."

What observability would have caught it faster. The OOM report names the victim, its RSS, and the constraint immediately; alerting on memory.events (oom_kill) would have made the page self-diagnosing. The fix is outside this lesson (raise the limit, or shrink the working set) — the diagnosis is what transfers.

check.sh verifies the cgroup OOM reproduces (constraint=CONSTRAINT_MEMCG) and names the victim. A later facilitator layer grades your evidence.
